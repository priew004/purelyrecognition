Title of Study: Perception of Natural Stimuli Under Occlusion

Researcher: Jett Priewe

Institution: University of Minnesota, Kersten Lab

Purpose of the Study: This study aims to understand how people make sense of the world around them, even when parts of what they see are hidden or unclear. For example, when you see someone walking behind a fence, your brain can still figure out where they are and what they’re doing. We want to learn more about how this works.

What You’ll Do: You will sit at a computer and look at images of objects. Some parts of these images will be hidden behind cut-outs, or apertures, of another image. You’ll be asked to make decisions about what you see. Each session will last less than 30 minutes.

Risks and Benefits: There are no risks to participating in this study. By taking part, you’ll help us better understand how the human brain processes visual information, which could contribute to advancements in science and technology.

Confidentiality: Your responses will be kept private. All data will be stored securely, and no personally identifiable information will be shared. If the data is shared with other researchers, it will be completely anonymous.

Voluntary Participation: Your participation is completely voluntary. You can stop at any time without any penalty.

Contact Information: If you have any questions, please contact Jett Priewe at priew004@umn.edu.
